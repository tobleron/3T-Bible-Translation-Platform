HOLY BIBLE
Edition: Tell The Truth | Version: 0.5 | Date: May 2025

MATTHEW Chapter 1

The Record of the Ancestry of Jesus Christ
1. The Record of the Ancestry of Jesus Christ, Son of David, Son of Abraham.
2. Abraham fathered Isaac, Isaac fathered Jacob, Jacob fathered Judah and his brothers.
3. Judah fathered Perez and Zerah by Tamar, Perez fathered Hezron, Hezron fathered Ram.
4. Ram fathered Amminadab, Amminadab fathered Nahshon, Nahshon fathered Salmon.
5. Salmon fathered Boaz by Rahab, Boaz fathered Obed by Ruth, Obed fathered Jesse.
6. Jesse fathered David the king. David fathered Solomon by the wife of Uriah.
7. Solomon fathered Rehoboam, Rehoboam fathered Abijah, Abijah fathered Asa.
8. Asa fathered Jehoshaphat, Jehoshaphat fathered Joram, Joram fathered Uzziah.
9. Uzziah fathered Jotham, Jotham fathered Ahaz, Ahaz fathered Hezekiah.
10. Hezekiah fathered Manasseh, Manasseh fathered Amon, Amon fathered Josiah.
11. Josiah fathered Jeconiah and his brothers at the time of the Babylonian exile.
12. After the Babylonian exile, Jeconiah fathered Shealtiel, Shealtiel fathered Zerubbabel.
13. Zerubbabel fathered Abiud, Abiud fathered Eliakim, Eliakim fathered Azor.
14. Azor fathered Zadok, Zadok fathered Achim, Achim fathered Eliud.
15. Eliud fathered Eleazar, Eleazar fathered Matthan, Matthan fathered Jacob.
16. Jacob fathered Joseph, husband of Mary, by whom Jesus was born—called Christ.
17. So from Abraham to David: fourteen generations; from David to the Babylonian exile: fourteen generations; and from the exile to Christ: fourteen generations.

The Birth of Jesus Christ
18. This is how Jesus Christ was born: Before Mary and Joseph came together, she was found to have a child from the Holy Spirit.
19. Joseph, being a righteous man and unwilling to disgrace her, resolved to divorce her quietly.
20. While he pondered this, an angel of the Lord appeared in a dream, saying, "Joseph, son of David, do not fear to take Mary as your wife, for her child is from the Holy Spirit."
21. She will bear a son, and you shall name him Jesus, for he will save his people from their sins.
22. All this happened to fulfill what the Lord said through the prophet:
23. "Behold, the virgin will conceive and bear a son, and they will call him Immanuel" (meaning "God with us").
24. When Joseph awoke he took Mary as his wife, as the angel of the Lord commanded.
25. He did not touch her until she gave birth to a Son, and he named him Jesus.

MATTHEW Chapter 2

The Visit of the Magi
1. After Jesus was born in Bethlehem of Judea during the days of King Herod, Magi from the east came to Jerusalem.
2. They said, “Where is the one born King of the Jews? We saw his star rise and have come to worship him.”
3. When Herod heard this, he was troubled, and all Jerusalem with him.
4. He gathered the chief priests and scribes and asked where the Christ was to be born.
5. “In Bethlehem of Judea,” they replied, “for this is what the prophet wrote:”
6. “And you, Bethlehem, in Judah, are by no means least among Judah’s rulers, for from you will come a ruler to shepherd my people Israel.”
7. Herod secretly called the Magi and found out exactly when the star had appeared.
8. He sent them to Bethlehem and said, “Search carefully for the child. When you find him, tell me so I too may worship him.”
9. After hearing the king, they went on their way, and the rising star guided them and stopped over where the child was.
10. When they saw the star, they rejoiced with intense joy!
11. They entered the house, saw the child with Mary his mother, and bowed to him in worship. Then they opened their treasures and presented him with gifts; gold, frankincense, and myrrh.
12. Being warned in a dream not to return to Herod, they went home by another route.

Refuge in Egypt
13. After they left, an angel of the Lord appeared in a dream to Joseph, saying, “Rise, take the child and his mother, flee to Egypt, and stay there until I tell you. Herod is seeking the child to kill him.”
14. So he rose, took the child and his mother at night, and fled to Egypt.
15. He stayed there until Herod died, to fulfill what the Lord spoke through the prophet: “Out of Egypt I called my son.”

The Weeping of Rachel
16. When Herod realized the Magi had outwitted him, he flew into a rage and ordered all boys two and under in Bethlehem and its vicinity to be killed, based on the timing he had learned from them.
17. To fulfill what was spoken through the prophet Jeremiah:
18. “A voice was heard in Ramah, weeping and deep mourning, Rachel weeping for her children. She refused to be comforted, because they are no more.”

The Return to Nazareth
19. After Herod died, an angel of the Lord appeared in a dream to Joseph in Egypt,
20. “Rise, take the child and his mother, go to the land of Israel, for those who sought the child’s life are now dead.”
21. He rose, took the child and his mother, and entered Israel.
22. But when he heard Archelaus ruled Judea in place of his father Herod, he was afraid to go. Being warned in a dream, he withdrew to the district of Galilee,
23. and he settled in a town called Nazareth, to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet: “He will be called a Nazarene.”

MATTHEW Chapter 3

The Ministry of John the Baptist
1. In those days, John the Baptist came proclaiming in the wilderness of Judea,
2. “Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near.”
3. This is the one spoken of by the prophet Isaiah: “A voice crying in the wilderness: ‘Prepare the way of the Lord, make his paths straight.’”
4. John wore a camel-hair garment with a leather belt around his waist, and ate locusts and wild honey.
5. Jerusalem, all Judea, and the whole Jordan region went out to him,
6. and they were baptized by him in the Jordan River, confessing their sins.
7. When he saw many Pharisees and Sadducees coming to be baptized, he said, “You brood of vipers! Who warned you to flee from the coming wrath?”
8. Bear fruit worthy of repentance.
9. Do not say to yourselves, ‘We have Abraham as our father,’ for I tell you that God can raise up children for Abraham from these stones.
10. The axe is already at the root of the trees. Every tree that does not bear good fruit will be cut down and thrown into the fire.
11. I baptize you with water for repentance, but one who comes after me is mightier than I, and I am not worthy to carry his sandals. He will baptize you with the Holy Spirit and fire.
12. His winnowing fork is in his hand. He will clear his threshing floor, gather his wheat into the barn, and burn the chaff with unquenchable fire.

The Baptism of Jesus
13. Then Jesus came from Galilee to the Jordan to be baptized by John.
14. But John tried to stop him, saying, “I need to be baptized by you, and you come to me?”
15. Jesus answered, “Let it be for now, for it is right to fulfill all righteousness.” Then John allowed him.
16. Right after Jesus was baptized, he came up from the water, and the heavens opened to him. He saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove and resting upon him.
17. A voice from heaven said, “This is my beloved son, in whom I am well pleased.”

MATTHEW Chapter 4

The Temptation of Jesus
1. Jesus was led by the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted by the devil.
2. After fasting forty days and nights, he was hungry.
3. The tempter said, “If you are the Son of God, command these stones to become bread.”
4. Jesus answered, “It is written: ‘Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word from the mouth of God.’”
5. The devil took him to the holy city and set him on the temple’s pinnacle.
6. “If you are the son of God,” he said, “throw yourself down. For it is written: ‘He will command His angels concerning you,’ and ‘they will lift you up in their hands, so you will not strike your foot against a stone.’”
7. Jesus answered, “It is also written: ‘You shall not test the Lord your God.’”
8. Again the devil took him to a very high mountain, showed him all the world’s kingdoms and their splendor.
9. “All these I will give you if you bow down and worship me,” he said.
10. Jesus said, “Be gone, Satan! For it is written: ‘Worship the Lord your God and Him only you shall serve.’”
11. Then the devil left him, and angels came and served him.

Jesus Begins His Ministry
12. When Jesus heard John was arrested, he departed to Galilee.
13. He left Nazareth and lived in Capernaum by the sea, in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali.
14. To fulfill what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah:
15. “Land of Zebulun, land of Naphtali, way of the sea, beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the nations.”
16. “The people sitting in darkness saw a great light, and to those sitting in the shadow of death, light has dawned.”
17. From then Jesus began to preach: “Repent, for the kingdom of heaven is near.”

Fishers of People
18. Walking by the Sea of Galilee, he saw two brothers: Simon called Peter, and Andrew, casting a net, for they were fishermen.
19. “Come, follow me, and I will make you fishers of people.”
20. At once, they left their nets and followed him.
21. Going on, he saw James and John in the boat with their father Zebedee, mending nets. He called them.
22. At once, they left the boat and their father and followed him.
23. He went through Galilee, teaching in synagogues, preaching the kingdom, and healing every disease and sickness.
24. His fame spread throughout Syria, and they brought to him all the sick, those with various diseases and torments, the demon-possessed, epileptics, and paralytics, and he healed them.
25. Large crowds followed him from Galilee, the Decapolis, Jerusalem, Judea, and beyond the Jordan.

MATTHEW Chapter 5

The Beatitudes
1. Seeing the crowds, Jesus went up the mountain. When he sat down, his disciples came to him.
2. He opened his mouth and taught them, saying:
3. Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.
4. Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted.
5. Blessed are the meek, for they will inherit the earth.
6. Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they will be filled.
7. Blessed are the merciful, for they will be shown mercy.
8. Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God.
9. Blessed are the peacemakers, for they will be called sons of God.
10. Blessed are those persecuted for righteousness’ sake, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven.
11. Blessed are you when people insult, persecute, and say all evil things against you because of me.
12. Rejoice and be glad, for great is your reward in heaven. For in the same way they persecuted the prophets before you.

Salt and Light
13. You are the salt of the earth. But if the salt loses its preserving power, how can it be restored? It is no longer good for anything, except to be thrown out and trampled underfoot.
14. You are the light of the world. A city set on a hill cannot be hidden.
15. No one lights a lamp and puts it under a basket, but on a stand, and it shines to all in the house.
16. In the same way, let your light shine before others, so they see your good works and glorify your Father in heaven.

Christ Fulfills the Law
17. Do not think I came to abolish the Law or the Prophets. I came not to abolish but to fulfill.
18. Truly I tell you, until heaven and earth pass away, not the smallest letter or stroke will pass from the Law until all is fulfilled.
19. Whoever breaks the least command and teaches others to do so will be called least in the kingdom of heaven. But whoever obeys and teaches them will be called great in the kingdom of heaven.
20. Unless your righteousness surpasses that of the scribes and Pharisees, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven.

Anger and Reconciliation
21. You have heard it said to the ancients: “Do not murder,” and “Whoever murders will be liable to judgment.”
22. But I tell you, whoever is angry with a dear one is liable to judgment. Whoever insults a dear one is liable to the court, and whoever says, 'You godless one!' is liable to the fire of Gehenna.
23. If you are offering your gift at the altar and remember a dear one holds something against you,
24. leave your gift there, go be reconciled with them, then come offer your gift.
25. Settle quickly with your accuser while you are on the way, or your accuser may hand you to the judge, then to the officer, and you will be thrown into prison.
26. Truly I tell you, you will not get out until You have paid the last penny.

Lust and Inner Purity
27. You have heard it said, “Do not commit adultery.”
28. But I tell you, anyone who looks at a woman with lust in mind has already committed adultery with her in his heart.
29. If your right eye causes you to sin, tear it out and throw it away. It is better to lose one part than for your whole body to be thrown into Gehenna.
30. If your right hand causes you to sin, cut it off and throw it away. It is better to lose one part than for your whole body to go into Gehenna.

Divorce
31. It was said, “Any man who divorces his wife must give her a certificate of divorce.”
32. But I tell you, any man who divorces his wife except for sexual covenant treachery makes her commit adultery. And any man who marries a divorced woman commits adultery.

Oaths
33. You have heard it said to the ancients, “Do not swear falsely, but keep your oaths to the Lord.”
34. But I tell you, do not swear at all. Neither by heaven, for It is God’s throne,
35. nor by the earth, for It is His footstool, or by Jerusalem, for It is the city of the great King.
36. And do not swear by your head, because you cannot make a single hair white or black.
37. Let your word be ‘Yes’ or ‘No’ for anything more comes from the evil one.

Do Not Retaliate
38. You have heard it said, ‘Eye for eye, tooth for tooth.’
39. But I tell you, do not resist an evil person. If anyone slaps you on the right cheek, turn the other to them also.
40. If anyone sues you for your shirt, give them your coat also.
41. If anyone forces you to go one mile, go with them two.
42. Give to whoever asks, and do not turn away from the one who wants to borrow.

Love Your Enemies
43. You have heard it said, ‘Love your neighbor and hate your enemy.’
44. But I tell you, love your enemies, bless those who curse you, do good to those who hate you, and pray for those who persecute you.
45. So you may be children of your Father in heaven. He makes his sun rise on the evil and the good, and sends rain on the righteous and the unrighteous.
46. If you love those who love you, what reward will you have? Do not even tax-collectors do the same?
47. If you greet only your own, what are you doing more than others? Do not even the Gentiles do the same?
48. Be perfect therefore, as your Father in heaven is perfect.

MATTHEW Chapter 6

Virtue in Secrecy
1. Beware of almsgiving to impress others; then you will have no reward from your Father in heaven.
2. When you give, do not broadcast publicly or in synagogues like hypocrites seeking praise from people. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward.
3. But when you give, do not let your left hand know what your right hand is doing.
4. Then your giving will be secret, and your Father who sees what is done in secret will repay you openly.
5. When you pray, do not perform in public like hypocrites who love to be seen. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward.
6. But when you pray, go into your room, shut the door, and pray in secret to your Father, for He who sees what is done in secret will reward you openly.

The Lord's Prayer
7. When you pray, do not repeat mindlessly like pagans who think they will be heard because of their many words.
8. Do not be like them, for your Father knows what you need before you ask.
9. Pray like this: Our Father in heaven, holy is your name.
10. Your kingdom come, your will be done on earth as it is in heaven.
11. Give us this day our daily bread.
12. Forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors.
13. And lead us not into temptation, but deliver us from evil. For yours is the kingdom, and the power, and the glory forever, Amen.
14. If you forgive people’s trespasses, your Father in heaven will forgive you.
15. But if you do not forgive others, your Father in heaven will also not forgive you.

Fasting
16. When you fast, do not look gloomy like the hypocrites, for they disfigure their faces to be seen fasting by people. Truly I tell you, they have received their reward.
17. But when you fast, anoint your head and wash your face,
18. so your fasting may not be seen by people but by your Father in secret; and your Father who sees what is done in secret will reward you openly.

The Real Treasure
19. Do not pile up treasures on earth, where moth and rust destroy, and where thieves break in and steal.
20. But store up treasures in heaven, where neither moth nor rust destroys, and where thieves do not break in or steal.
21. For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.

Body of Light
22. The eye is the lamp of the body. If your eye is pure, your whole body will be full of light.
23. But if your eye is evil, your whole body will be full of darkness. If then the light you rely on is darkness, how deep is that darkness.

Serving Two Masters
24. No one can serve two masters. Either you will hate one and love the other, or be devoted to one and despise the other. You cannot serve God and Mammon.

Living the Present Moment
25. Therefore I tell you, do not worry about your life, what you will eat or drink, or about your body, and what you will wear. Is not life more than food, and the body more than clothing?
26. Look at the birds of the air. They do not sow, or reap, or gather into barns, yet your Father in heaven feeds them. Are you not much more valuable than they?
27. Who among you by worrying can add a single hour to their life?
28. And why do you worry about clothing? Consider how the lilies of the field grow; they do not labor or spin,
29. yet I tell you, not even Solomon in all his splendor was clothed like one of these.
30. If God so clothes the grass of the field, which is here today and tomorrow is thrown into the oven, will He not much more clothe you; you of little faith?
31. Therefore, do not worry, saying, “What will we eat, drink, or wear?”
32. For the Gentiles chase these things, and your Father in heaven knows you need them.
33. Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things will be added to you.
34. Therefore, do not worry about tomorrow, for tomorrow will carry its own burdens. Each day has enough trouble of its own.

MATTHEW Chapter 7

Honor the Word
1. Judge not, or you too will be judged.
2. And with the measure you use, it will be measured back to you.
3. Why do you see the speck in your brother’s eye, but miss the log in your own?
4. How can you say to your sister, “Let me remove the speck from your eye,” while a log is still in yours?
5. You hypocrite! First remove the log from your own eye, then you’ll see clearly to take the speck from a fellow believer’s eye.
6. Do not give what is holy to dogs, or throw your pearls before swine, lest they trample them underfoot, turn and tear you to pieces.

Ask, Seek, Knock
7. Ask, and it will be given to you; seek, and you will find; knock, and it will be opened to you.
8. For everyone who asks receives, and the one who seeks finds, and to the one who knocks, it will be opened.
9. Who among you, if his son asks for bread, would give him a stone?
10. Or if his daughter asks for a fish, would give her a serpent?
11. If you, being evil, yet know how to give good gifts to your children, how much more will your Father in heaven give good things to those who ask Him?

The Golden Rule
12. Whatever you want others to do for you, do the same for them. This is foundational in the Law and the Prophets.

The Narrow and Wide Gates
13. Enter through the narrow gate, for the gate is wide and the road is broad that leads to destruction, and many enter through it.
14. For the gate is narrow and the road is hard that leads to life, and the few are they who find it.

True and False Prophets
15. Beware of false prophets, who come to you in sheep’s clothing but inwardly are ravenous wolves.
16. By their fruit, you will recognize them. Are grapes gathered from thorns, or figs from thistles?
17. Every good tree bears good fruit, but an evil tree bears bad fruit.
18. A good tree cannot bear evil fruit, and an evil tree cannot bear good fruit.
19. Every tree that does not bear good fruit is cut down and thrown into the fire.
20. It follows, by their fruit you will recognize them.

True and False Disciples
21. Not everyone who says to me, “Lord, Lord,” will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only the one who does the will of my Father in heaven.
22. Many will say to me on that day, “Lord, Lord, did we not prophesy in your name, cast out demons in your name, and perform many mighty works in your name?”
23. Then I will tell them plainly, “I never knew you. Depart from me, you workers of lawlessness.”

The Wise and Foolish Builders
24. Therefore, everyone who hears these words and lives by them is like a wise man who built his house on the rock.
25. The rain fell, the rivers rose, the winds blew and beat against that house, yet it did not fall, for it had been founded on the rock.
26. But everyone who hears these words and does not do them is like a foolish man who built his house on the sand.
27. The rain fell, the rivers rose, the winds blew and struck that house, and it fell, and mighty was its fall.

The Crowds React to Jesus
28. When Jesus finished speaking, the crowds were astonished at His teaching.
29. For he taught with authority, not like their scribes.

MATTHEW Chapter 8

Jesus Cleanses a Leper
1. When Jesus came down the mountain, great crowds followed him.
2. Suddenly, a leper came and knelt before him. “Lord, if you will, you can make me clean.”
3. Jesus reached out, touched him, and said, “I will; be cleansed.” Immediately his leprosy was gone.
4. Then Jesus said, “Tell no one, but go show yourself to the priest and offer the gift Moses commanded, as testimony to them.”

The Faith of the Centurion
5. When Jesus entered Capernaum, a centurion came and pleaded with him.
6. “Lord, my servant lies at home paralyzed and in terrible pain.”
7. Jesus said, “I will come and heal him.”
8. But the centurion replied, “Lord, I am not worthy to have you under my roof. Just say the word, and my servant will be healed.”
9. For I too am a man under authority, with soldiers under me. I say to one, ‘Go,’ and he goes; ‘Come,’ and he comes; ‘Do this,’ and he does it.
10. Jesus was amazed and said to those following, “Truly I tell you, I have not found such great faith in all Israel.”
11. Many will come from east and west to feast with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob in the kingdom of heaven.
12. But the sons of the kingdom will be banished to the outer darkness, where there is weeping and gnashing of teeth.
13. Then Jesus said to the centurion, “Go. As you believed, so let it be done for you.” And his servant was healed that very hour.

The Healer At Home
14. Jesus entered Peter’s house and saw his mother-in-law sick in bed with a fever.
15. He touched her hand, and the fever left her. She rose and began to serve them.
16. That evening they brought him many who were demon-oppressed. He cast out the spirits with a word and healed all the sick.
17. To fulfill what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah: “He took our sicknesses and bore our diseases.”

Follow The Living God
18. When Jesus saw a great crowd around him, he gave orders to cross to the other side.
19. A scribe came and said, “Teacher, I will follow you wherever you go.”
20. Jesus replied, “Foxes have dens, and birds of the air have nests, but the Son of Man has nowhere to lay his head.”
21. Another disciple said, “Lord, let me first go and bury my father.”
22. But Jesus said, “Follow me, and let the dead bury their own dead.”

Jesus Calms the Storm
23. When Jesus got into the boat, his disciples followed him.
24. Suddenly, a great storm rose on the sea, and the boat was being swamped by the waves. But Jesus was asleep.
25. The disciples came and woke him, saying, “Lord, save us! We are perishing!”
26. He said to them, “Why are you afraid, you of little faith?” Then he got up and rebuked the winds and the sea, and there was a great calm.
27. The men were amazed and said, “What kind of man is this? Even the winds and the sea obey him!”

Jesus Heals Two Demon-Possessed Men
28. When he came to the other side, to the region of the Gergesenes, two demon-possessed men met him, coming out of the tombs. They were so violent that no one could pass that way.
29. Suddenly, they cried out, “What do you want with us, Jesus, Son of God? Have you come to torment us before the appointed time?”
30. A large herd of pigs was feeding some distance away.
31. The demons begged him, “If you cast us out, allow us to go into the herd of pigs.”
32. He said to them, “Go.” So they came out and went into the pigs, and the whole herd rushed down the steep bank into the sea and drowned.
33. Those tending them fled and went into the city and reported everything, including what happened to the demon-possessed men.
34. Suddenly, the whole city came out to meet Jesus. When they saw him, they begged him to leave their region.

MATTHEW Chapter 9

Authority to Forgive Sins
1. Jesus stepped into a boat, crossed over, and came to his town.
2. Suddenly, some men brought him a paralyzed man on a mat. Seeing their faith, Jesus said, “Take heart child, your sins are forgiven.”
3. Some scribes said to themselves, “He is blaspheming.”
4. Jesus knew their thoughts and said, “Why think evil in your hearts?”
5. Which is easier to say, ‘Your sins are forgiven,’ or ‘Rise and walk’?
6. But so you know the Son of Man has authority to forgive sins, he said to the paralyzed man, “Rise, pick up your mat and go home.”
7. He rose and went home.
8. When the crowd saw this, they were awestruck and praised God who had given such authority to humankind.

Mercy, Not Sacrifice
9. As Jesus passed by, he saw a man named Matthew at the tax booth. “Follow me,” he said. And Matthew got up and followed him.
10. While Jesus was eating at the house, many tax collectors and sinners came and ate with him and his disciples.
11. When the Pharisees saw this, they said to his disciples, “Why does your teacher eat with tax collectors and sinners?”
12. Jesus heard and said, “It is not the healthy who need a physician, but the sick.”
13. Go and learn what this means: ‘I desire mercy, not sacrifice.’ I came not to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance.

The Old Cannot Bear the New
14. Then John’s disciples came and asked, “Why do we and the Pharisees fast often, while your disciples do not?”
15. Jesus said, “Can wedding guests mourn while the bridegroom is with them? Days will come when he is taken away from them; then they will fast.”
16. No one puts a patch of unshrunk cloth on an old garment, for it tears away and makes the rip worse.
17. No one pours new wine into old wineskins. If they do, the skins burst, the wine spills, and both are lost. New wine is poured into fresh wineskins, and both are preserved.

Rise, Little Girl
18. While he was speaking, a synagogue official came and knelt before him. “My daughter has just died,” he said. “But come, lay your hand on her, and she will live.”
19. Jesus got up and followed him, and so did his disciples.
20. Suddenly, a woman who had suffered bleeding for twelve years came up behind him and touched the edge of his cloak.
21. For she said to herself, “If I only touch his cloak, I will be healed.”
22. Jesus turned, saw her, and said, “Take heart, daughter. Your faith has saved you.” And the woman was healed from that moment.
23. When Jesus entered the official’s house, he saw the flute players and the uproar of mourning.
24. He said to them, “Leave, for the girl is not dead but asleep.” And they laughed at him.
25. When the crowd was sent out, Jesus went in, took her by the hand, and the girl rose.
26. News of this spread throughout all that region.

Faith That Sees
27. As Jesus went on from there, two blind men followed him, crying out, “Have mercy on us, Son of David!”
28. When he entered the house, the blind men came to him. Jesus said, “Do you believe I am able to do this?” They said, “Yes, Lord.”
29. Then he touched their eyes and said, “According to your faith, let it be done to you.”
30. Their eyes were opened, and Jesus sternly warned them, “See that no one knows.”
31. Yet they went out and spread the news about him throughout all that region.

Like Sheep Without a Shepherd
32. As they were leaving, a mute demon-possessed man was brought to Jesus.
33. When the demon was cast out, the mute man spoke. The crowds were amazed and said, “Nothing like this has ever been seen in Israel!”
34. But the Pharisees said, “He casts out demons by the ruler of demons!”
35. Jesus went through all the towns and villages, teaching in their synagogues, preaching the good news of the kingdom, and healing every kind of disease and affliction.
36. When he saw the crowds, he ached with compassion for them, because they were weary and scattered, like sheep without a shepherd.
37. Then he said to his disciples, “The harvest is plentiful, but the workers are few.”
38. Therefore pray to the Lord of the harvest to send out workers into his harvest.

MATTHEW Chapter 10

The Chosen Twelve
1. Jesus called his twelve disciples and gave them authority to drive out unclean spirits and heal every disease and affliction.
2. These are the names of the twelve apostles: first, Simon (called Peter), and Andrew his brother; James son of Zebedee, and John his brother;
3. Philip and Bartholomew; Thomas and Matthew the tax collector; James son of Alphaeus, and Thaddaeus;
4. Simon the Zealot; and Judas Iscariot, the one who also betrayed him.

Go Without Fear
5. Jesus sent out these twelve, commanding them: “Do not go among the Gentiles or enter any Samaritan town;
6. go instead to the lost sheep of Israel.
7. As you go, proclaim: ‘The kingdom of heaven has come near.’
8. Heal the sick, cleanse lepers, raise the dead, cast out demons. Freely you have received; freely give.
9. Do not take gold, silver, or copper for your belts;
10. no bag for the road, no second tunic, no sandals, no staff; for the worker is worthy of his provisions.

Entering Homes & Towns
11. When you enter any town or village, find out who is worthy and stay there until you leave.
12. As you enter the house, greet it.
13. If the house is worthy, let your peace come upon it; but if it is not, let your peace return to you.
14. If anyone does not welcome you or listen to your words, shake the dust off your feet as you leave that house or town.
15. Truly I tell you, it will be more bearable for Sodom and Gomorrah on judgment day than for that town.

Sheep Among Wolves
16. Look, I am sending you out like sheep among wolves; so be wise as serpents and innocent as doves.
17. Watch out for people. They will deliver you over to courts and flog you in their synagogues.
18. You will be brought before governors and kings because of me, as a testimony to them and to the nations.
19. Do not worry about how or what you should say; it will be given to you at that moment.
20. You will not be the one speaking, but the Spirit of your Father speaking through you.
21. A family member will betray another to death, and a father his child; children will rise against their parents and have them killed.
22. You will be hated by everyone because of my name, but the one who endures to the end will be saved.
23. When they persecute you in one town, flee to another. Truly I tell you, you will not finish going through the towns of Israel before the Son of Man comes.

Fear God, Not Man
24. A disciple is not greater than their teacher, nor a slave greater than their master.
25. It is enough for a disciple to strive to become like his teacher, and a slave to be like his master. If they called the head of the house ‘Beelzebul,’ how much more they would for the members of his household?
26. Therefore do not fear them. Nothing is hidden that will not be revealed, or a secret that will not be known.
27. What I tell you in the dark, speak in the light; what you hear whispered, proclaim from the rooftops.
28. Do not fear those who kill the body but cannot kill the soul. Rather, fear the One who can destroy both the soul and the body in Gehenna.
29. Are not two sparrows sold for a penny? Yet not one of them falls to the ground apart from your Father.
30. Even the hairs of your head are all numbered.
31. Therefore, do not fear. You are worth more than many sparrows.

Do You Deny the Truth?
32. Whoever acknowledges me before others, I will also acknowledge before my Father in heaven.
33. But whoever denies me before others, I will also deny before my Father in heaven.

The Sword of Truth
34. Do not think I came to bring peace to the earth. I did not come to bring peace, but a sword.
35. I came to divide a man against his father, a daughter against her mother, a daughter-in-law against her mother-in-law.
36. A person’s enemies will be members of their own household.
37. Anyone who loves father or mother more than me is not worthy of me; and anyone who loves son or daughter more than me is not worthy of me.
38. Whoever does not take up their cross and follow me is not worthy of me.
39. Whoever finds their life will lose it, and whoever loses their life for my sake will find it.

Whoever Receives You, Receives Me
40. Whoever receives you receives me, and whoever receives me receives Him who sent me.
41. Whoever receives a prophet because they are a prophet will receive a prophet’s reward, and whoever receives a righteous person because they are righteous will receive a righteous person’s reward.
42. And whoever gives even a cup of cold water to one of these little ones because they are my disciple, truly I tell you, they will never lose their reward.

MATTHEW Chapter 11

Are You The One?
1. When Jesus finished instructing his twelve, he moved on to teach and preach in their towns.
2. But John, hearing in prison about the works of Christ, sent two of his disciples
3. to ask him, ‘Are you the one to come, or should we expect another?’
4. Jesus replied, ‘Go tell John what you hear and see:
5. the blind see, the lame walk, lepers are cleansed, the deaf hear, the dead are raised, and the poor are preached the good news.’
6. ‘Blessed is the one who does not turn away from me.’

John, The Last Prophet
7. As they departed, Jesus spoke to the crowds about John: ‘What did you go out to the wilderness to see? A reed shaken by the wind?’
8. ‘Or a man in soft clothing? Behold, those who wear fine garments live in kings’ palaces.’
9. ‘But what did you go out to see? A prophet? Yes, and more than a prophet.’
10. ‘Behold, I send my messenger ahead of you, who will prepare your way before you.’
11. ‘Truly I tell you, among those born of women, none has risen greater than John the Baptist, yet the least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than he.’
12. ‘From the days of John the Baptist until now, the kingdom of heaven has been advancing forcefully, and the zealous take hold of it.’
13. ‘For all the prophets and the law prophesied until John.’
14. ‘And if you are willing to accept it, he is Elijah who was to come.’
15. ‘Whoever has ears, let them listen.’

Children of Complaint
16. ‘To what shall I compare this generation? It is like children sitting in the marketplace, calling to their companions,’
17. ‘We played the flute for you, and you did not dance; we sang a dirge, and you did not mourn.’
18. ‘For John came neither eating nor drinking, and they say, ‘He has a demon.’
19. ‘The Son of Man came eating and drinking, and they say, ‘Look, a glutton and a drunkard, a friend of tax collectors and sinners.’ Yet wisdom is justified by her deeds.’

Unrepentant Hearts
20. Then he began to rebuke the cities where most of his mighty works had been done, because they did not repent.
21. ‘Grief upon you Chorazin and Bethsaida! For if the miracles done in you had been done in Tyre and Sidon, they would have repented long ago in sackcloth and ashes.’
22. ‘But I tell you, it will be more bearable for Tyre and Sidon on the day of judgment than for you.’
23. ‘And you, Capernaum, will you be exalted to heaven? No, you will be brought down to Hades. For if the miracles done in you had been done in Sodom, it would have remained to this day.’
24. ‘But I tell you, it will be more bearable for the land of Sodom on the day of judgment than for you.’

Mysteries to Little Children
25. At that time, Jesus declared, ‘I praise you, Father, Lord of heaven and earth, because you have hidden these things from the wise and learned and revealed them to little ones.’
26. ‘Yes, Father, for this was your gracious will.’
27. ‘All things have been handed over to me by my Father. No one knows the Son except the Father, and no one knows the Father except the Son and those to whom the Son wills to reveal Him.’

Come to Me, I Will Give You Rest
28. ‘Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest.’
29. ‘Share my labor upon you and learn from me, for I am gentle and humble in heart, and you will find rest for your souls.’
30. ‘For my way is freeing and my burden is light.’

MATTHEW Chapter 12

Lord of the Sabbath
1. Jesus walked through grainfields one Sabbath. His disciples were hungry, so they plucked the heads of grain and ate.
2. The Pharisees objected, “Look, your disciples are doing what is not lawful on the Sabbath!”
3. He said to them: “Have you not read what David did when he was hungry, and those beside him,”
4. how he entered the house of God and ate the bread of the Presence, which only priests were permitted to eat?
5. Or have you not read in the Law that the priests in the temple break the Sabbath and are still innocent?
6. But I tell you, something greater than the temple is here.
7. If you had known the meaning of, “I desire mercy, not sacrifice,” you would not have condemned the innocent.
8. For the Son of Man is Lord even of the Sabbath.

No Wrong Time for Good
9. Jesus left there and entered their synagogue.
10. A man with a withered hand was there. They asked him, “Is it lawful to heal on the Sabbath?” hoping to accuse him.
11. He replied, “Who among you, if you had a sheep that fell into a pit on the Sabbath, would not take hold of it and lift it out?”
12. Therefore, it is lawful to do good on the Sabbath.”
13. Then he said to the man, “Stretch out your hand.” He stretched it out, and it was fully restored like the other.
14. But the Pharisees went out and plotted how to destroy him.

The Chosen Servant
15. Jesus, aware of this, withdrew. Many followed him, and he healed them all.
16. He warned them not to make him known,
17. to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet Isaiah:
18. “Behold my chosen servant, my beloved in whom my soul is well-pleased. I will put my Spirit upon him, and he will proclaim justice to the nations.”
19. He will not argue or cry out, nor will his voice be heard in the streets.
20. A bruised reed he will not break, a smoldering wick he will not quench, until he leads justice to victory.
21. And in his name the nations will hope.

A Divided Kingdom
22. They brought him a demon-possessed man who was blind and mute. Jesus healed him, and the man spoke and saw.
23. The crowds were amazed and said, “Could this be the Son of David?”
24. But the Pharisees, seeing this, said, “This man casts out demons only by Beelzebul, the ruler of demons.”
25. Jesus, knowing their thoughts, said, “Every kingdom divided against itself is destroyed, and every house divided cannot stand.”
26. If Satan drives out Satan, he is divided against himself. How then can his kingdom stand?
27. If I cast out demons by Beelzebul, by whom do your own people cast them out? They will be your judges.
28. But if I cast out demons by the Spirit of God, then the kingdom of God has come upon you.
29. How can anyone enter a strong man’s house and take his possessions unless he first binds the strong man? Then he can plunder his house.
30. Whoever is not with me is against me, and whoever does not gather with me scatters.

The Unforgivable Sin
31. Therefore I tell you, every sin and blasphemy can be forgiven, but blasphemy against the Spirit will not be forgiven.
32. Whoever speaks against the Son of Man will be forgiven, but whoever speaks against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven, in this age or in the one to come.

Known by Your Fruit
33. Make the tree good, and its fruit will be good; make the tree bad, and its fruit will be bad, for a tree is known by its fruit.
34. You brood of vipers! How can you speak good, being evil? For out of the overflow of the heart, the mouth speaks.
35. A good person brings forth good from the treasure of a good heart, and an evil person brings forth evil from an evil heart.
36. But I tell you, every careless word people speak will be accounted for on the day of judgment.
37. For by your words you will be justified, and by your words you will be condemned.

The Sign of Jonah
38. Then some of the scribes and Pharisees said, “Teacher, we want to see a sign from you.”
39. He replied, “An evil and adulterous generation demands a sign, but none will be given except the sign of the prophet Jonah.
40. For just as Jonah was in the belly of the great fish for three days and three nights, so will the Son of Man be in the heart of the earth for three days and three nights.
41. The people of Nineveh will stand at the judgment with this generation and condemn it, for they repented at Jonah’s preaching, and behold, something greater than Jonah is here.
42. The queen of the South will rise at the judgment with this generation and condemn it, for she came from the ends of the earth to hear Solomon’s wisdom, and behold, something greater than Solomon is here.

Unclean Spirits Return
43. When an unclean spirit leaves a person, it wanders through waterless places seeking rest but finds none.
44. Then it says, ‘I will return to my house from which I came.’ When it returns, it finds the house empty, swept, and put in order.
45. Then it goes and brings with it seven other spirits more evil than itself, and they enter and settle there. The final state of that person is worse than the first. So it will be with this wicked generation.

My True Family
46. While he was still speaking to the crowds, his mother and brothers stood outside, wanting to speak with him.
47. Someone said, “Look, your mother and brothers are standing outside, wanting to speak with you.”
48. But he replied to the one who told him, “Who is my mother, and who are my brothers?”
49. Stretching out his hand toward his disciples, he said, “Here are my mother and my brothers.
50. For whoever does the will of my Father in heaven is my brother, sister, and mother.

MATTHEW Chapter 13

The Parable of the Farmer
1. That day, Jesus left the house and sat by the sea.
2. Large crowds gathered around him, so he got into a boat and sat down, while the crowd stood on the shore.
3. He spoke to them many things in parables: “A farmer went out to sow seed.”
4. As he sowed, some seed fell along the path, and the birds came and devoured it.
5. Other seed fell on rocky ground, where it sprang up quickly, having little soil.
6. But when the sun rose, it was scorched, and without roots, it withered.
7. Other seed fell among thorns, and the thorns grew up and choked it.
8. Yet other seed fell on good soil and produced a crop, some a hundredfold, some sixty, some thirty.
9. Whoever has ears, let them listen.

Why Jesus Speaks in Parables
10. The disciples came to him and asked, “Why do you speak to them in parables?”
11. He answered, “Because the secrets of the kingdom of heaven have been given to you, but not to them.”
12. For those who have will be given more, and they will have abundance. But those who do not have, even what they have will be taken away.
13. This is why I speak to them in parables: Though seeing, they do not see; hearing, they do not listen or understand.
14. In them the prophecy of Isaiah is fulfilled: ‘You will hear and never understand; you will see and never perceive.’
15. For this people’s hearts have grown hard; they barely hear with their ears, and they have closed their eyes. Otherwise, they might see with their eyes, hear with their ears, understand with their hearts, and turn, and I would heal them.
16. But blessed are your eyes, for they see, and your ears, for they hear.
17. For truly I tell you, many prophets and righteous people longed to see what you see but did not see it, and to hear what you hear but did not hear it.

The Parable of the Farmer Explained
18. Listen to what the parable of the farmer means.
19. When someone hears the word of the kingdom but does not understand it, the evil one comes and snatches away what was sown in their heart. This is the seed along the path.
20. The seed on rocky ground is the one who hears the word and instantly receives it with joy,
21. but since it has no root, it lasts only a short time. When trouble or persecution comes because of the word, they quickly fall away.
22. The seed among thorns is the one who hears the word, but the worries of this age and the deceitfulness of wealth choke the word, making it unfruitful.
23. But the seed on good soil is the one who hears the word and understands it, bearing fruit, yielding a hundredfold, sixty, or thirty.

The Parable of the Weeds
24. He presented another parable to them, saying, “The kingdom of heaven is like a farmer who sowed good seed in his field.”
25. But while everyone slept, his enemy came and sowed weeds among the wheat and went away.
26. When the seed sprouted and produced grain, the weeds also appeared.
27. The servants of the landowner came to him and said, ‘Master, didn’t you sow good seed in your field? Where did these weeds come from?’
28. He replied, ‘An enemy did this.’ The servants asked, ‘Do you want us to pull them up?’
29. ‘No,’ he said, ‘lest you uproot the wheat with them.’
30. Let both grow together until the harvest. Then I will tell the harvesters, ‘First gather the weeds and tie them in bundles to be burned, then gather the wheat into my barn.’

The Parables of the Mustard Seed and the Leaven
31. He presented another parable to them, saying, “The kingdom of heaven is like a mustard seed that a man planted in his field.”
32. Although it is the smallest of all seeds, when it grows, it becomes the largest of garden plants and forms a tree, and the birds of the air come and nest in its branches.
33. He told them another parable: “The kingdom of heaven is like yeast that a woman took and worked into three measures of flour, a large amount, until the whole batch of dough was fully leavened and risen.”

Jesus Fulfills Prophecy by Speaking in Parables
34. Jesus spoke all these things to the crowds in parables. He said nothing to them without a parable,
35. to fulfill what was spoken through the prophet: “I will open my mouth in parables; I will declare things hidden since the foundation of the world.”

The Parable of the Weeds Explained
36. Then he left the crowds and went into the house. His disciples came to him and asked, “Explain to us the parable of the weeds in the field.”
37. He answered, “The man who sows the good seed is the Son of Man.”
38. The field is the world, and the good seed represents the children of the kingdom. The weeds are the children of the evil one,
39. and the enemy who sowed them is the devil. The harvest is the end of the age, and the harvesters are angels.
40. Just as the weeds are gathered and burned in the fire, so it will be at the end of the age.
41. The Son of Man will send out his angels, and they will gather from his kingdom all causes of sin and the workers of lawlessness,
42. and throw them into the blazing furnace, where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth.
43. Then the righteous will shine like the sun in the kingdom of their Father. Whoever has ears, let them listen.

The Hidden Treasure and the Pearl
44. The kingdom of heaven is like treasure hidden in a field. When someone found it, they hid it again, and in their joy, they went and sold all they had and bought that field.
45. Again, the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant seeking fine pearls.
46. When they found one of great value, they went away, sold all they had, and bought it.

The Parable of the Net
47. Again, the kingdom of heaven is like a net let down into the sea, gathering fish of every kind.
48. When it was full, they pulled it up on the shore, sat down, and collected the good fish into baskets, but the bad they threw away.
49. So it will be at the end of the age: the angels will come and separate the wicked from among the righteous
50. and throw them into the blazing furnace, where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth.

Treasures New and Old
51. Jesus said to them, "Have you understood all these things?" They replied, "Yes, Lord."
52. He said to them, "Therefore, every scribe trained for the kingdom of heaven is like the master of a house who brings out of his treasure both new and old things."

Rejected in His Hometown
53. When Jesus had finished these parables, he left that place.
54. Coming to his hometown, he taught them in their synagogue, and they were astonished, saying, "Where did this man get this wisdom and these mighty works?"
55. Is not this the carpenter's son? Is not his mother called Mary, and his brothers James, Joseph, Simon, and Judas?
56. And are not all his sisters with us? Where then did this man get all these things?
57. And they took offense at him. But Jesus said to them, "A prophet is not without honor except in his hometown and in his own household."
58. And he did not do many miracles there because of their unbelief.

MATTHEW Chapter 14

The Death of John the Baptist
1. At that time, Herod the tetrarch heard the news about Jesus
2. and said to his servants, “This is John the Baptist. He has risen from the dead, and that is why these miraculous powers are at work in him.”
3. For Herod had arrested John, bound him, and put him in prison because of Herodias, his brother Philip’s wife,
4. because John had kept telling him, “It is not lawful for you to have her.”
5. Although he wanted to kill him, he feared the people, for they considered John a prophet.
6. But on Herod’s birthday, the daughter of Herodias danced before the company and pleased Herod,
7. so he promised with an oath to give her whatever she asked.
8. At her mother’s urging, she said, “Give me the head of John the Baptist here on a platter.”
9. Although the king was distressed, because of his oaths and guests, he ordered it to be given.
10. He sent and had John beheaded in prison.
11. His head was brought on a platter, given to the girl, and she took it to her mother.
12. John’s disciples came, took the body, buried it, then went and reported to Jesus.

Feeding the Five Thousand
13. When Jesus heard this, he withdrew by boat to a solitary place, but the crowds, hearing of it, followed him on foot from the towns.
14. When he came ashore, he saw a great crowd, felt compassion for them, and healed their sick.
15. As evening approached, the disciples came to him and said, “This is a remote place, and the hour is late. Send the crowds away to the villages to buy food for themselves.”
16. But Jesus said, “They do not need to go. You give them something to eat.”
17. They said, “We have only five loaves and two fish.”
18. He said, “Bring them here to me.”
19. He instructed the crowds to sit on the grass. Taking the five loaves and two fish, he looked up to heaven, blessed them, broke them, and gave them to the disciples, who shared them with the crowds.
20. They all ate and were satisfied, and the disciples gathered twelve full baskets of leftovers.
21. Those who ate were about five thousand men, besides women and children.

Jesus Walks on Water
22. Immediately, Jesus made his disciples get into the boat and go ahead of him to the other side, while he dismissed the crowds.
23. Right after he went up on the mountain by himself to pray. When evening came, he was there alone.
24. Meanwhile, the boat was already amid the sea, battered by the waves, for the wind was against it.
25. In the fourth watch of the night, Jesus came to them, walking on the sea.
26. When the disciples saw him walking on the sea, they were terrified and said, “It is a ghost!” and cried out in fear.
27. But immediately Jesus said to them, “Take heart! It is I. Do not be afraid.”
28. Peter answered, “Lord, if it is you, command me to come to you on the water.”
29. “Come,” he said. Then Peter got down out of the boat, walked on the water, and came toward Jesus.
30. But when he saw the wind, he was afraid and, beginning to sink, cried out, “Lord, save me!”
31. Immediately, Jesus reached out his hand, took hold of him, and said, “You of little faith, why did you doubt?”
32. And when they climbed into the boat, the wind ceased.
33. Then those in the boat came and worshiped him, saying, “Truly, you are the Son of God.”

Healing in Gennesaret
34. When they had crossed over, they came to the region of Gennesaret.
35. When the people of that place recognized him, they sent word throughout the surrounding region and brought to him all who were sick.
36. They begged him to let them touch just the edge of his cloak, and all who touched it were healed.

MATTHEW Chapter 15

Truth Versus Tradition
1. Then the scribes and Pharisees from Jerusalem came to Jesus, saying: 
2. “Why do your disciples break the tradition of the elders? They do not wash their hands before they eat.”
3. He answered, “And why do you break the commandment of God for the sake of your tradition?”
4. For God said, ‘Honor your father and mother,’ and, ‘Whoever curses father or mother must be put to death.’
5. But you say, ‘If anyone declares to their parents, “What you might have received from me is a gift to God,”
6. they are not obligated to honor their father or mother.’ You have nullified the word of God for the sake of your tradition.
7. Hypocrites! Isaiah rightly prophesied about you, saying:
8. ‘This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me.
9. In vain do they worship me, teaching as doctrines the commandments of men.’

What Defiles a Person
10. He called the crowd and said, “Listen and understand:
11. It is not what goes into the mouth that defiles a person; this defiles a person.”
12. Then his disciples came and said, “Do you know the Pharisees were offended by this statement?”
13. He answered, “Every plant my Father has not planted will be uprooted.”
14. “Leave them alone. They are blind guides leading the blind, and if the blind lead the blind, both will fall into a pit.”
15. Then Peter said, “Explain this parable to us.”
16. Jesus said, “Are you also still without understanding?”
17. “Do you not see that whatever enters the mouth goes into the stomach and is expelled?”
18. “But what comes out of the mouth comes from the heart, and this defiles a person.”
19. “For out of the heart come evil thoughts, murder, adultery, sexual immorality, theft, false witness, and slander.”
20. “These are what defile a person, but eating with unwashed hands does not defile a person.”

The Faith of a Canaanite Woman
21. Jesus left there and went to the region of Tyre and Sidon.
22. A Canaanite woman from that region came out, crying, “Have mercy on me, Lord, Son of David! My daughter is severely tormented by a demon.”
23. But he did not answer her. His disciples urged him, “Send her away, for she keeps crying out after us.”
24. He replied, “I was sent only to the lost sheep of the house of Israel.”
25. But she came and knelt before him, saying, “Lord, help me!”
26. He replied, “It is not right to take the children’s bread and throw it to the dogs.”
27. “Yes, Lord,” she said, “but even the dogs eat the crumbs that fall from their masters’ table.”
28. Then Jesus said, “Dear woman, great is your faith! Let it be done for you as you desire.” And her daughter was healed from that very hour.

Healing the Multitudes
29. Jesus walked along the Sea of Galilee, went up the mountain, and sat down.
30. Great crowds came to him, bringing the lame, the blind, the crippled, the mute, and many others. They laid them at his feet, and he healed them.
31. The crowd marveled as they saw the mute speaking, the crippled made whole, the lame walking, and the blind seeing. And they praised the God of Israel.

Feeding the Four Thousand
32. Then Jesus called his disciples and said, “I have compassion for the crowd, because they have already been with me three days and have nothing to eat. I do not want to send them away hungry, lest they collapse on the way.”
33. His disciples said, “Where can we get enough bread in this desolate place to feed such a crowd?”
34. Jesus asked, “How many loaves do you have?” They replied, “Seven, and a few small fish.”
35. He instructed the crowd to sit on the ground.
36. Taking the seven loaves and the fish, he gave thanks, broke them, and gave them to the disciples, who shared them with the crowd.
37. They all ate and were satisfied, and the disciples collected seven full baskets of leftovers.
38. Those who ate were four thousand men, besides women and children.
39. After sending the crowd away, he got into the boat and went to the region of Magadan.

MATTHEW Chapter 16

Demand a Sign
1. The Pharisees and Sadducees approached to test Him, asking for a sign from heaven.
2. He replied, “When evening comes, you say, ‘Fair weather, for the sky is red,’
3. and in the morning, ‘Stormy weather, for the sky is red and overcast.’ You know how to interpret the sky but fail to discern the signs of the times.
4. An evil and adulterous generation seeks a sign, but none will be given except the sign of Jonah.” Then he left them and went away.

Blind to the Bread of Heaven
5. When the disciples crossed to the other side, they forgot to bring bread.
6. Jesus said to them, “Watch and beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees.”
7. They discussed this among themselves, saying, “It is because we did not bring any bread.”
8. Aware of this, Jesus said, “You of little faith, why are you talking about having no bread?
9. Do you still not understand? Do you not remember the five loaves for the five thousand, and the full baskets you gathered?
10. Or the seven loaves for the four thousand, and how many baskets you gathered?
11. How do you not understand that I was not speaking about bread? Beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees.”
12. Then they understood that he was not talking about bread, but about the teaching of the Pharisees and Sadducees.

The Rock Declares Jesus as the Christ
13. When Jesus came to the region of Caesarea Philippi, he asked his disciples, “Who do people say the Son of Man is?”
14. They replied, “Some say John the Baptist, others Elijah, and still others Jeremiah or one of the prophets.”
15. He said to them, “But who do you say I am?”
16. Simon Peter answered, “You are the Christ, the Son of the living God.”
17. Jesus replied, “Blessed are you, Simon son of Jonah, for this was not revealed to you by flesh and blood, but by my Father in heaven.
18. And I tell you that you are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades will not overcome it.
19. I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven; whatever you bind on earth will be bound in heaven, and whatever you loose on earth will be loosed in heaven.”
20. Then he commanded his disciples not to tell anyone that he was the Christ.

Jesus Foretells his Suffering
21. From that time, Jesus began to tell his disciples that he must go to Jerusalem, suffer many things from the elders, chief priests, and scribes, be killed, and be raised on the third day.
22. Then Peter took Him aside and began to rebuke Him, saying, “Far be it from you, Lord! This will never happen to you!”
23. But Jesus turned and said to Peter, “Get behind me, Satan! You are a stumbling block to me, for you are not mindful of the things of God, but the things of people.”

The Cost of Discipleship
24. Then Jesus said to his disciples, “If anyone desires to come after me, let them deny themselves, take up their cross, and follow me.”
25. For whoever wants to save their life will lose it, but whoever loses their life for my sake will find it.
26. For what will it profit someone to gain the whole world if they lose their soul? Or what can someone give in exchange for their soul?
27. For the Son of Man will come in the glory of his Father with his angels, and then he will reward each one according to their deeds.
28. Truly, I tell you, some standing here will not taste death until they see the Son of Man coming in his kingdom.

MATTHEW Chapter 17

The Transfiguration
1. After six days, Jesus took Peter, James, and his brother John up a high mountain alone.
2. He was transfigured before them; his face shone like the sun, and his clothes became as bright as light.
3. And behold, Moses and Elijah appeared, talking with him.
4. Peter said to Jesus, “Lord, it is good for us to be here; if you wish, let us set up three shelters: one for you, one for Moses, and one for Elijah.”
5. While he was still speaking, behold, a bright cloud overshadowed them, and a voice from the cloud said, “This is my beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased. Listen to him!”
6. When the disciples heard this, they fell facedown to the ground, terrified.
7. But Jesus came, touched them, and said, “Rise, do not be afraid.”
8. When they lifted up their eyes, they saw no one but Jesus alone.

Elijah Has Come Already
9. As they were coming down the mountain, Jesus commanded them, “Tell no one about the vision until the Son of Man is risen from the dead.”
10. His disciples asked him, “Why then do the scribes say that Elijah must come first?”
11. Jesus replied, “Indeed, Elijah comes first and will restore all things.”
12. “But I tell you, Elijah has already come, and they did not recognize him but did to him as they pleased. In the same way, the Son of Man will also suffer at their hands.”
13. Then the disciples understood that he was speaking to them about John the Baptist.

Healing a Demon-Possessed Boy
14. When they came to the crowd, a man approached Jesus, knelt before him, and said,
15. “Lord, have mercy on my son, for he is an epileptic, suffers terribly, and often falls into fire or water.”
16. “I brought him to your disciples, but they could not heal him.”
17. Jesus said, “You faithless and twisted generation, how long must I be with you? How long must I bear with you? Bring him here to me.”
18. Jesus rebuked the demon, and it came out of him, and the boy was healed from that very hour.
19. Then the disciples came to Jesus privately and asked, “Why could we not cast it out?”
20. Because of your little faith. Truly I tell you, if you have faith as small as a mustard seed, you can say to this mountain, ‘Move from here to there,’ and it will move. Nothing will be impossible for you.
21. [“But this kind only comes out by prayer and fasting.”]

The Coming Resurrection
22. While they were staying in Galilee, Jesus said to them, “The Son of Man is about to be betrayed into the hands of men,”
23. “and they will kill him, and on the third day he will be raised up.” And they were deeply grieved.

The Temple Tax
24. When they came to Capernaum, the collectors of the temple tax approached Peter and asked, “Does your teacher not pay the temple tax?”
25. He replied, “Yes.” When he entered the house, Jesus anticipated him, saying, “What do you think, Simon? From whom do the kings of the earth collect taxes or customs, from their own sons or from outsiders?”
26. Peter answered, “From outsiders.” Jesus said to him, “Then the sons are free.”
27. “But so we do not offend them, go to the sea, cast a hook, and take the first fish you catch. When you open its mouth, you will find a four-drachma coin. Take it and give it to them for me and you.”

MATTHEW Chapter 18

The Greatest in the Kingdom
1. At that time the disciples came to Jesus and asked, “Who is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven?”
2. He called a child and placed the child in their midst.
3. He said, “Truly, I tell you, unless you change and become like little children, you will never enter the kingdom of heaven.”
4. Therefore, whoever humbles like this child is the greatest in the kingdom of heaven.
5. And whoever receives a little one like this child in my name receives me.

Temptations to Sin
6. But whoever causes any of these little ones who believe in me to sin, it would be better for them to have a millstone hung around their neck and be drowned in the depths of the sea.
7. Grief upon the world for the temptations it causes, for temptations must come, but doomed is the person through whom they come!
8. If your hand or foot causes you to stumble, cut it off and throw it away. It is better to enter life maimed or crippled than to have two hands or two feet and be thrown into the eternal fire.
9. And if your eye causes you to stumble, gouge it out and throw it away. It is better to enter life with one eye than to have two eyes and be thrown into the fire of Gehenna.

The Parable of the Lost Sheep
10. See that you do not despise one of these little ones, for I tell you, their angels in heaven always see the face of my Father in heaven.
11. For the Son of Man has come to save the lost.
12. What do you think? If a man has a hundred sheep and one of them goes astray, will he not leave the ninety‑nine on the hills and go to find the one that went astray?
13. And if he finds it, truly I tell you, he rejoices more over that one than over the ninety‑nine that did not stray.
14. In the same way, it is not the will of your Father in heaven that any of these little ones should perish.

Confronting a Dear One in Sin
15. If a dear one sins against you, go and point out their fault between you and them alone. If they listen to you, you have won them over.
16. But if they will not listen, take one or two others along, so that every accusation may be confirmed by the testimony of two or three witnesses.
17. If they refuse to listen to them, share it with the church. And if they refuse to listen even to the church, treat them as you would a Gentile or a tax collector.
18. Truly I tell you, whatever you restrain on earth will be restrained in heaven, and whatever you set free on earth will be set free in heaven.
19. Again, truly I tell you, if two of you agree on earth about anything they ask, it will be done for them by my Father in heaven.
20. For where two or three gather in my name, there I am in their midst.

The Parable of the Unforgiving Servant
21. Then Peter came to him and asked, “Lord, how many times should I forgive a dear one if they sin against me? Up to seven times?”
22. Jesus said to him, “I do not say up to seven times, but up to seventy times seven.”
23. Therefore, the kingdom of heaven is like a king who wanted to settle accounts with his servants.
24. As he began to settle, a servant who owed him ten thousand talents was brought to him.
25. Since he could not pay, his master ordered that he, his wife, his children, and all he had be sold to repay the debt.
26. The servant fell down before him and said, ‘Master, have patience with me, and I will repay you everything.’
27. The master of that servant took pity on him, released him, and forgave the debt.
28. But that servant went out and found one of his fellow servants who owed him a hundred denarii. He seized him and began to choke him, saying, ‘Pay me what you owe!’
29. His fellow servant fell at his feet and begged him, ‘Have patience with me, and I will repay you.’
30. But he refused and threw him into prison until he could pay back what he owed.
31. When his fellow servants saw what happened, they were deeply grieved and went to their master and reported all that had happened.
32. Then his master called him and said, ‘You wicked servant! I forgave all that debt because you begged me.’
33. Should you not have shown mercy to your fellow servant, just as I showed mercy to you?
34. In his anger, his master handed him over to be tortured until he paid all he owed.
35. So also my Father in heaven will do to you if each of you does not forgive your brothers and sisters from the heart.
