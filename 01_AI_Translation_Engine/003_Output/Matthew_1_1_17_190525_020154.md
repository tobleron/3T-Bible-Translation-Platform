# Step 3: Comprehensive Interpretation Report

This passage provides a genealogical record of Jesus Christ, tracing His lineage back to Abraham through King David. It emphasizes Jesus’ fulfillment of God's covenantal promises with Abraham (Genesis 12:1–3) and David (2 Samuel 7:12–16), placing Him within both the royal and patriarchal lines of Israel.

The structure is highly symbolic. The division into three groups of fourteen generations reflects the numerical significance of "seven" in biblical numerology (14 = 7 × 2), symbolizing completeness and divine order. This aligns with earlier prophetic patterns, such as the seven years of famine in Egypt or the seven-day creation.

Verse 6 has particular theological weight due to its reference to Solomon being born from the wife of Uriah, which alludes to the sin of David recorded in 2 Samuel 11. This may be included to emphasize that even within the royal line, imperfection exists—yet Jesus emerges as the perfect Son of David.

Early church fathers like Irenaeus emphasized this genealogy as a proof of Jesus' messianic legitimacy, while Augustine saw it as evidence of the grace of God working through flawed human ancestry to bring about salvation.

If Jesus were to evaluate this interpretation, He would likely approve it with a score of **9/10**. The passage accurately reflects His lineage and purpose but could have been more focused on the theological implications rather than merely listing names. However, the inclusion of historical context and symbolic meaning is strong.
