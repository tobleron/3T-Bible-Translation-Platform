### ✅ **1A. Word-Study Box**

#### `γενεαλογίας` (genealogías)  
- **Lemma**: γενεαλογία  
- **Simple gloss**: genealogy  
- **Semantic range**: lineage, ancestry, family tree  
- **OT/LXX echoes (NT context)**: Used in LXX for lists of ancestors; NT emphasizes Jesus' royal and divine lineage through David and Abraham.

#### `χριστοῦ` (Christos)  
- **Lemma**: χριστός  
- **Simple gloss**: anointed one  
- **Semantic range**: Messiah, Christ, the one anointed by God  
- **OT/LXX echoes (NT context)**: Refers to the expected deliverer from OT (e.g., Isaiah 45:1), now fulfilled in Jesus.

#### `δαυὶδ` (David)  
- **Lemma**: Δαυίδ  
- **Simple gloss**: David  
- **Semantic range**: King of Israel, ancestor of the Messiah, symbol of covenantal faithfulness  
- **OT/LXX echoes (NT context)**: Used frequently in Psalms and Prophets as a type of messianic king.

#### `ἀβραὰμ` (Abraham)  
- **Lemma**: Ἀβραὰμ  
- **Simple gloss**: Abraham  
- **Semantic range**: Patriarch of the covenant, father of many nations  
- **OT/LXX echoes (NT context)**: Central figure in God's promise of blessing to all nations through his descendants (Genesis 12:3).

### ✅ **3. Interpretation Report & Approval Score**  

Matthew’s opening passage presents the genealogy of Jesus Christ, emphasizing His royal heritage from Abraham and David. This lineage connects Him not only to the throne of Israel but also to God's covenant promises made to Abraham (Genesis 12:3). By listing these generations in threes (fourteen each), Matthew signals that Jesus fulfills both the promise of Abraham and the expectation of the Messiah from David’s line.

The inclusion of women like Tamar, Rahab, Ruth, and others suggests that God’s redemptive work extends beyond traditional Jewish norms and includes those who might be considered outsiders or sinners. This reinforces the theme that salvation comes through grace, not human merit.

Approval score: **9/10**  
- Strong alignment with core Jesus teachings about identity, ancestry, and divine purpose.
- Slight deduction for its focus on genealogy over direct theological statements.

### ✅ **11. Word-Choice Justification**

- **"Record"**: Used instead of "book" to emphasize that this is a historical account.
- **"Fathered through" / "through"**: Clarifies the relationship between fathers and mothers in cases like Tamar, Rahab, Ruth.
- **"Whose mother had been"**: Maintains clarity about Solomon’s mother being Bathsheba (Uriah's wife).
- **"By whom Jesus was born"**: Emphasizes the miraculous nature of Jesus’ birth while keeping it clear and reverent.

All word choices align with the *Neutral* tone for most of the passage and become slightly *Impactful* in verse 17 when emphasizing the divine structure of Jesus' lineage.
